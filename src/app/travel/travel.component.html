City Center

Granada has a beautiful historic center. Although prices here may be a bit higher for accommodation, we recommend this area as you can walk everywhere. Reference streets: Recogidas Street, Gran Vía de Colón, Elvira Street, and Reyes Católicos Street.

Google Maps
Realejo Neighborhood

10 min walk from the city center and closer to the most touristic areas. Here you can enjoy a very authentic neighborhood known for its squares, low houses, and bohemian life. Reference streets: Plaza Fortuny, Pavaneras Street, Molinos Street, and Campo del Príncipe Street.

Google Maps
Gran Capitán Area

A bit further from the center, 15 min walk, but with very good connections and access. Besides being an area where accommodation will be cheaper, it is a quiet area with lots of life. Reference streets: Gran Capitán Street, San Juan de Dios Street, and Tablas Street.

Google Maps
Albaicín

The most touristic neighborhood in Granada. Depending on the area you choose, it may be a bit far from our wedding celebration, but if your intention is to enjoy and discover Granada during those days, it is also a great option. Warning: this neighborhood is in the highest part of Granada, so there are hills. Reference streets: Carrera del Darro and Calderería Nueva Street.

Google Maps
Lavadero Viewpoint

Located in the Realejo neighborhood, here you can enjoy one of the best sunsets in the city, and the best part is that it’s not crowded at all ;)

Sacromonte Abbey

A beautiful place with an 18th-century chapel located on the Sacromonte hill in Granada. With privileged views, the abbey is one of the most magical places in Granada. For the more adventurous, there is a beautiful circular hike going up through the Albaicín and passing by the Sacromonte caves. If not, you can easily get there by taxi.

Alhambra

The crown jewel of Granada. The Nasrid palace built in 1384 that has witnessed more than 100 years of Granada’s history. Don’t miss it!

Cathedral

In the heart of the city is Granada’s cathedral. Gothic style and built in 1589, it is one of the most charming areas of Granada. We recommend strolling around, eating in Plaza del Aguador, and having a coffee in the lovely cafés nearby.
